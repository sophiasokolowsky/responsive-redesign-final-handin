Why Is There Need For Redesign!?

Take a look at the images below or you can also click on the link and see the full original Trader Joe's wesbite. What comes to mind upon first seeing the layout?

Visit Trader Joe's

Below you will find some information describing what I found the design issues to be... click on the box to see more!

The search bar is a small icon in the top right corner which is difficult to find quickly. The homepage has two navigation bars - a side panel with limited options and a dropdown above it which is confusing. The focus of the page is cleaning products, which may mislead users since it is a grocery store website. This could lead to users not using the page efficiently or maybe not even using the page at all!

The page lacks a clear visual hierarchy, which makes it hard for users to distinguish different sections. Clickable elements don't change on hover, making it unclear whether they are clickable or decorative. This makes it difficult for users to learn how to navigate the website.

The lack of a clear grocery store theme makes it difficult for users to remember the site's purpose. A cluttered layout with mixed information complicates navigation, making it hard for users to remember how to find specific sections.

The website's conceptual model does not align with user expectations for a grocery store. Instead, it resembles a newspaper or blog, with a confusing focus on cleaning products. This misalignment creates confusion and hinders its usability.

The website's general design issues have a negative impact on its efficiency, learnability, and memorability, making its functionality less intuitive. While WebAIM WAVE detected no errors, I think some text is too small, such as the subheadings, which if larger, could improve section differentiation. Although there are no contrast issues, the color scheme could be improved to create a more appealing and digestible webpage.
